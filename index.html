About Little Bamiyan

Little Bamiyan, named after the historic and culturally significant city in Afghanistan, is a vibrant cultural precinct located on Thomas Street in Dandenong. This thriving marketplace represents the significant contributions of the Hazara community to the City of Greater Dandenong.

Since the late 1990s, our community has transformed this area from a quiet street into a bustling economic center filled with authentic restaurants, traditional bakeries, specialty grocery stores, and various cultural businesses that showcase our rich heritage.

Little Bamiyan serves as a gathering place where people from all backgrounds come together to experience authentic cuisine, shop for traditional goods, and participate in cultural events that celebrate diversity and foster community connections.

Visit Little Bamiyan

How to Find Us

Little Bamiyan is located on Thomas Street in Dandenong, approximately 30km southeast of Melbourne CBD.

Public Transport

Dandenong Station is within walking distance (approximately 10 minutes), served by multiple train lines and bus routes from central Melbourne.

Parking

Street parking is available on Thomas Street and surrounding areas. Public parking lots are located within a short walking distance.

Best Times to Visit

The bazaar is liveliest on weekends, especially Saturday mornings. Most businesses are open seven days a week, with restaurants open until late evening.
